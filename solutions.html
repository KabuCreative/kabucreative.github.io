Conversion Optimisation

Improve your website to generate more business

Conversion optimisation is the process of improving your website to generate more leads and/or sales for your business.

It requires a thorough understanding of your business alongside an in-depth analysis of your current campaign's performance statistics.

We then apply our knowledge of marketing, usability and industry best practices to help increase your conversion rate.

We have a number of strategies to help your website or campaign perform better, but these are by far the most commonly appointed:

Landing pages

Landing pages are one page lead generation websites designed for specific marketing campaigns (i.e. an email or adwords campaign).

By sending your prospective buyers to a 'landing page' instead of your main website, you can tailor the message to that particular audience.

Landing pages are designed to:

  • Deliver a strong, lean marketing message that differentiates you from your competition
  • Include a prominent 'call to action', urging your users to submit an enquiry
  • Be highly relevant to the search term, ad, or marketing campaign they are receiving traffic from
  • Establish trust & credibility with your prospective customer
  • Address customer doubts and remove enquiry 'road blocks'

More often than not, the combination of a focussed message and the correct implementation of the above results in a higher rate of enquiry and an increased return on your online marketing investment.

Conversion testing

Conversion testing works by sending a portion of your visitors to an alternative variation of a webpage, to find out if this page generates a better result.

A 'better result' could be a higher rate of enquiry, more products being added to a shopping cart, or a higher rate of purchase.

Tests are set up in specialist software that measures the new page's performance against the original.

Once one page is determined the 'winner' (and statistical significance is reached), this variation goes on to be the new base variation for the next test. In this manner, the performance of your page increases over time.

There are several software solutions available that make the technical process of setting up a test child's play. Where the real specialisation lies is in understanding when, what and where to test. Common testing parameters include:

  • Headlines
  • Images
  • Calls to action (positioning, colour and wording of purchase buttons and/or enquiry forms)
  • Body copy / text
